## 2. The Fintech Angle: PCAF v3 + Financed Emissions

This is the **critical strategic insight** that makes CarbonIQ relevant to fintech.

### The Problem Banks Face (As of Dec 2025)
- PCAF v3 now requires banks to report **embodied carbon of buildings** in their financed emissions
- For most banks, financed emissions (Scope 3) = **99%+ of total carbon footprint**
- Construction loans, commercial real estate, mortgages — all need embodied carbon data
- **633+ financial institutions** have joined PCAF globally
- Banks have NO automated way to get this data from construction projects

### What CarbonIQ Already Does
- Parses BOQs → classifies materials → calculates tCO₂e per ISO 21930
- Identifies 80% significant materials (Pareto analysis)
- Handles unit conversion, density lookups, thickness extraction
- AI-powered classification (ECCS 6-step hierarchy)
- Audit trail for every calculation

### The Bridge: CarbonIQ as Bank Data Infrastructure
```
Construction Company → Uploads BOQ → CarbonIQ processes it
                                          ↓
                                    API returns:
                                    - Total embodied carbon (tCO₂e)
                                    - PCAF data quality score (1-5)
                                    - Material breakdown
                                    - 80% significant materials
                                    - Green/Transition/Brown classification
                                          ↓
Bank's Loan System ← Receives carbon risk data ← CarbonIQ API
                                          ↓
                              Bank reports financed emissions
                              per PCAF v3 standard
```

### Pitch Positioning
> **CarbonIQ: AI-Powered Embodied Carbon Intelligence for Construction Finance**
>
> Banks financing $15.5T in global construction have a blind spot: embodied carbon.
> With PCAF v3 now requiring financed emissions reporting on construction portfolios,
> CarbonIQ provides the AI-powered data layer that converts any Bill of Quantities
> into PCAF-compliant embodied carbon assessments — enabling green loan scoring,
> portfolio carbon risk monitoring, and regulatory compliance for construction
> lenders across APAC.

### EU CBAM Impact on APAC (Critical Driver)

**EU CBAM definitive phase started January 1, 2026:**
- Importers must now **purchase CBAM certificates** for cement, iron & steel, aluminium
- Iron & steel = **81% of total CBAM costs**
- Total costs could exceed **EUR 12B** (~15% of import value)
- Cement sector: costs could exceed **400% of import value** in high-price scenarios
- **Scope expansion proposed**: downstream steel/aluminium products (machinery, vehicles, construction equipment)
- **ASEAN impact**: Vietnamese, Indonesian, Malaysian exporters face pressure; paying domestic carbon price = deduction from CBAM levy
- Asian steel makers (Hyundai Steel, POSCO) retrofitting with electric furnaces for green steel

**Why this matters for CarbonIQ:** Construction is the END CONSUMER of CBAM-covered materials (steel, cement, aluminium). CarbonIQ's BOQ analysis provides the embodied carbon data that proves compliance or quantifies CBAM liability.

**Key gap:** There is **NO dominant APAC-specific construction embodied carbon platform**. The market is served by expensive global tools, limited government calculators, or general carbon accounting platforms that don't handle construction BOQs.
